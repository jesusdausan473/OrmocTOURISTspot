OrmocTOURistSpot

Welcome to OrmocTOURistSpot

This island province shies away from heavy tourism, making it ideal for tourists looking to ‘get off the beaten track’. Nevertheless, the island does boast several interesting historical landmarks, and unveils plenty of scenic spots to explore too.

This Eastern-Visayan island might not have the international reputation like Boracay and Palawan. However, it stills features a host of fascinating sites. Lake Danao welcomes outdoor-lovers year-round with its natural ambience and entertaining activities.

MOST VISITED

Several interesting historical landmarks

Veterans Centennial Park

The Veteran’s Park was constructed in early 1990s in honor of the Filipino soldiers who fought for the liberation of Leyte and the Philippines.The Centennial Marker was erected in 1998 to commemorate the 100th year of Philippine independence.One gets a good view of the bay,the port and the plaza from this point. It is a favorite rendezvous for the young and old alike for picnics and leisurely strolls on its verdant lawn. And from here, one can experience the refreshing breeze from the sea and witness the magnificent beauty of the sunset.

Ruins of Cong. Dominador Tan Residence

The residence of Cong. Dominador Tan with well-manicured lawn and a swimming pool was one of the most beautiful scenery in Ormoc at that time which was completed a year before the World War II.As war broke out, it was utilized as one of the headquarters of the Japanese military operations in Leyte and was partially destroyed by the bombings of the famous Battle of Ormoc Bay.Its ruins serve as a symbolic structure of Ormocanons indulgence of World War II.

DETAILS

Puente de la Riena

The "Bridge of the Queen" is considered as the last existing physical structure that symbolizes Spanish colonization of the city. Known as the oldest bridge in the city, its cobblestones are still intact, relics of an age long gone. It was built in the early 1800’s but was completed in 1861. During the Spanish era, the bridge served as a docking area for sailboats, vintas of Chinese, Javans and Indonesians who frequented the island to sell their produce. The bridge is still in use today. It is situated near the twin buildings of the Executive and Legislative branch of the city and beside the Old Ormoc City Hall.

Jose Rizal Monument

In front of the old capitol building of Ormoc City stands a statue of our national hero Dr. Jose Rizal.A modest park is also located in the same place.

Phil-Japan Peace Memorial

An impressive memorial marker put up by the Nagoya and Gifu Prefectures as a token of friendship of Japanese and Filipinos in the aftermath of World War II. It is situated in one of the city's picturesque hills overlooking Ormoc Bay, Carlota Hills in Brgy. Can-adieng. Japanese visitors frequented this area in memory of their beloved countrymen who fought in World War II.

Mass Grave

The graveyard of the victims of the 1991 flash flood is where the biggest cross in the city can be found. An obelisk in the center is erected where interpretations of the victims by the artists all over the country will be installed. A commemorative mass, lighting of candles and laying of flowers are done on this site every November 5th each year in memory of the victims who perished in the tragedy.

LAKE DANAO

The violin-shaped Lake Danao is a popular haunt for locals and tourists, thanks to its swag of natural highlights and entertaining activities. It is found in a mountainous pocket of Leyte, just a short distance from Ormoc.

Tourists can rent boats, go fishing, enjoy floating cottages, and plenty of other outdoor activities.

Alto Peak, The Tower of the East.
One of the best places in Ormoc.The locals are very much hospitable, especially the heads of the guide organization (the CALEGUID). the guide is very much approachable and well-trained. you summitted the mountain, Alto Peak, and back to the barangay in just a day. The trail is narrow, different from the mountains and the place is full of different flora and fauna which can only be seen in the area. The Lake Janagdan is a true beauty to behold, . Their are also falls, the Tres Aguas, you really enjoyed after the hike.

located at Cabintan Ormoc City

Lake Janagdan

Lake Janagdan is located in the eastern part of Ormoc and is part of Mt. Aminduen ranges. It measures 1120masl and is surrounded by lush and diverse vegetation. Various plant species can be found, with ferns, vines, and trees covered with mosses. Different types of insects often crawl along the trail too.

Sayahan Falls.

How to get there:

  • From Ormoc City, ride jeepney going to Brgy. Lake Danao at Ormoc Jeepney Terminal across Public Market and Ormoc City Port.
  • Drop off, and ride Habal-habal going to Sitio Maglahug.
  • Register at Gaas Tourism Office. Php20.00 entrance fee per pax and Php300.00 guide fee for 5 members in a group or below. For 6 guests or more, guide fee may vary for your own safety.
  • Be prepared, 1-2 hours trekking/hiking to Sayahan Falls, currently the local government is working on improvements for a better access to this hidden treasure. Rest assured, a true beauty is waiting.

Ormoc Bay

Ormoc Bay is a large bay in the island of Leyte in the Philippines. The bay is an extension of the Camotes Sea. The city of Ormoc lies at the head of the bay and exports rice, copra and sugar. The World War II Battle of Ormoc Bay took place from November 11 until mid-December in Ormoc Bay during late 1944.

The Battle of Ormoc Bay was a series of air-sea battles between Imperial Japan and the United States in the Camotes Sea in the Philippines from 11 November-21 December 1944, part of the Battle of Leyte in the Pacific campaign of World War II. The battles resulted from Japanese operations to reinforce and resupply their forces on Leyte and U.S. attempts to interdict them.

Hotel Don Filipe

The Hotel Don Filipe is the largest hotel in Ormoc City.It is located just beside the terminal and offers an excellent view of Ormoc Bay.Hotel Don Filipe is considered as the best hotel in Ormoc city.

Located at Larrazabal Street corner Bonifacio Street, Ormoc City

Ormoc Villa Hotel

Ormoc Villa Hotel is one of three hotels that are located in the main business area of the city. Ormoc Villa's distinctive look make it look more homely than the other two hotels (Pongos Hotel and Hotel Don Filife) which boast more of a traditional look.From the street Ormoc Villa as the name implies resembles a huge mansion. This hotel is situated about 250 meters to the east of the public vehicle terminal. A quick ride in a tricycle is in need if a tourist is not into walking. If they does decided to take the short walk from the terminal,they will actually be treated to a view of the Ormoc bay on their way to the hotel.

AI Lodge

The AI Lodge is one of the new inns in Ormoc City. Its is located just beside the vehicle terminal and the price for a night stay is very reasonable. If you want to stay in a comfy place but a bit tight on a budget, AI Lodge could be right for you.
